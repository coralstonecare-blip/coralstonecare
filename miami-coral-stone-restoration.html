Common surface problems

Miami Weather Is Hard on Porous Stone

Humidity, rain, salt air, irrigation, shade, and intense sun can quickly change the appearance and performance of exterior surfaces.

  • Dark mold and algae buildup
  • Weathered or uneven color
  • Cracks, chips, and worn joints
  • Stains and moisture exposure
  • Aging or failed sealer
  • Slippery organic buildup
A surface-specific plan helps avoid the damage caused by overly aggressive cleaning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is pressure washing safe for coral stone?

Coral stone is porous and can be damaged by excessive pressure. We select pressure, cleaners, distance, and technique according to the material and its current condition.

What surfaces do you work on?

We service coral stone, limestone, travertine, pool decks, patios, walkways, driveways, walls, and other exterior masonry surfaces.

Does every surface need to be sealed?

No. The recommendation depends on the material, prior treatments, moisture behavior, exposure, and how the area is used. We assess the surface before recommending a sealer.

How do I receive an estimate?

Submit the form with a short description. Photos can help us understand the condition, but some projects require an on-site evaluation before the final scope is confirmed.

How soon will you contact me?
